# Basic Rules

### Team Building Rules

Each Team Manager builds an arena team with common rarity creatures from all
first-party *Pathfinder 2nd Edition* sources. Each creature costs an amount of
XP equal to the XP awarded for its defeat against a party of players at the
arena event's
base level. Each arena event has a base level, usually an even value. If a
base level is not defined, the default is 2nd level.

Every creature must share at least one trait or keyword with every other
creature on the same team. Each creature must be given a unique name, and Team
Managers are
encouraged to roleplay the team dynamics.

All creatures enter combat with their weapons drawn and ammunition readied if
they wield a ranged weapon.

### Creature Restrictions

The following creature properties cannot be used in a standard arena event or
are disqualified if they abuse them.

- Creatures with etherealness (or similar abilities), or resistances high
  enough that a high-damage attack from a creature of event level - 1 would
  deal no damage to them (*GMG, p. 64*).
- Creatures with burrow and flight speed are disqualified if they fly higher
  than 20 feet off the ground, or burrow deeper than 10 feet under the ground.

### Hero Points

At the start of the first, second, and third rounds, each Team receives a Hero
Point. Hero Points can only be used to re-roll attacks and skill checks, not to
stabilize dying creatures.

### Creature Advancement

Death is final. When a creature is reduced to 0 Hit Points by a lethal attack,
it dies, and its corpse will be discarded after the fights. The Team Manager
can refill vacant spots in the team with any other creature within the
remaining budget XP.

Team Managers can agree to a friendly game where attacks that reduce a creature
to 0 Hit Points are treated as nonlethal damage. Doing so does not require a
-2 circumstance penalty to the attack. Creatures do not gain battle
experience from such fights. Arena
organizers may disallow friendly fights, as they do not please the crowd's
bloodthirst.

Each creature that survives 3 games becomes a veteran. Veteran creatures gain
+2 HP per creature level (min 1), +1 to each attack, damage, and skill check,
and +1 to each DC and AC. Abilities with a basic save or the requirement to use
again (such as 1d4 rounds) do +2 damage instead of +1.

Each veteran creature that survives 6 games becomes elite. Elite creatures gain
+5 HP per creature level (min 1), +2 to each attack, damage, and skill check,
and +2 to each DC and AC. Abilities with a basic save or the requirement to use
again (such as 1d4 rounds) do +4 damage instead of +2.

Team Managers are encouraged to reward their veterans and elites with
battle-earned names and must put the word Veteran and Elite before the
creature's name, as well as increase the size of their token by +10% (for
veteran) or 20% (for elite) over the base size.

### Team Fund

Some Arena Masters may grant Team Managers a gold stipend for equipment such as
magical items, runes, talismans, and more. The amount of gold granted to each
side depends on the arena event. Organizers can increase or decrease the event
level if they feel particularly stingy or generous, but always equally to both
teams.

# The Games

### Standard

There are no additional rules. Each team fights to the death for the
entertainment of the crowds. Surviving creatures are granted battle
experience and may become veteran or elite.

### Tag Team

Four teams walk into the arena, two paired on each side. The pair that defeats
all creatures of the opposing teams claims victory. However, the next fight
must be between both teams in the pair once they replenish their ranks with
new creatures. Both fights count as two for battle experience.

### Party Clash

Each team consists of four player characters of a level equal to the event
level, with a budget equal to the same event level.

### Leader

Each Team Manager can spend 40 XP to recruit one player character with a level
equal to the arena event to lead their creatures. If the Leader is killed, all
remaining team creatures become *frightened 2* recovered as usual.

**Tyrant.** Team Managers can spend 60 XP to recruit one player character with a
character level equal to the event level plus 2.

### Massacre

Each Team Manager must have three teams ready for battle. At the start of the
fourth and eighth turns, the gates open, and each team manager can deploy one
of their remaining teams onto the arena. If a team manager has no living
creatures in the arena at the start of a combat round, they are defeated.

**Rushed Wave.** Once per battle, each Team Manager can spend 2 Hero Points to
release one of their teams one turn earlier than normal.

**Blitz.** At the start of the third and sixth turns, the gates open, and each
team manager deploys the next team onto the arena.

### Clown Defense

Each Team Manager has a captive clown, who is given no equipment other than a
silly costume and cannot defend itself. The clown has AC and saves equal to 15
plus the event level, and 3 Hit Points per event level. It uses all its actions
to ridicule the opposing team or flee from threats.

As long as the clown is alive, at its initiative, it can ◆◆ attempt to rouse
the crowds and then make a flat check to earn its team 1 Hero Point. If the
clown's controlling team has more living creatures in the arena than the
opposing team, the DC is 15; otherwise, it is DC 12.

### King of the Hill

**Requires:** King of the Hill map.

As an additional win condition, any team that has one of its creatures end its
turn on the top of the crown-stamped 2x2 area for three consecutive turns
without being pushed outside the area or slain claims victory for its team.

![](https://www.gmbinder.com/images/HLbELSk.png)

The top platform is 12 feet tall and cannot be reached from the ground using
normal reach 10 ft. weapons.

### Monster Bash

**Requires:** Monster Pit map.

The two Teams face a captured monster run by an independent third player,
usually an elite-adjusted creature of a level equal to event level plus 3. The
monster is released through the pit at the start of 2nd turn. Both teams must
do their best to survive by defeating the deadly threat with the least of
losses. The team that delivers the death blow to the monster gains
immediately 2 Hero Points.

### Draft

The Arena Master provides six sets of four creatures each, and each team takes
turns picking one from that pool until the pool is empty. Each Team Manager
has three sets. The first to pick is decided by either the losing team from
the previous battle, if any, or with a dice roll.

Each set contains one uncommon rarity and three common rarity creatures with
same level and XP cost. Each Team Manager reveals one set at a time, picks one
creature from it, and then passes the remaining creatures to the opposing
team. Each list is passed three times until it contains no more creatures.

Each Team Manager then prepares their team from the twelve creatures they have
picked. The requirement that creatures must share the same type is ignored.

**Tag Team.** With four players, the Arena Master provides six sets of eight
creatures each. Each set contains two uncommon creatures; the rest are common
creatures with the same level and XP cost. Team Managers are put in a
crossseat, with each team manager facing an opposing team. The first, third,
and fifth sets are passed clockwise, and the second, fourth, and sixth are
passed counterclockwise. Each team manager should see each set twice on their
first and fifth picks.

**Lucky Drops.** The Arena Master places at random common or uncommon two Elite
Event Level +2 creatures in separate packs. This is done by rolling 1d48
twice (reroll if same results) and exchanging one of the creatures when
ordered from 1st to 48th position across all sets, with the Elite Event Level
+2 creature.

### Opponent Knowledge

Each Team Manager is assumed to have researched the strengths and weaknesses
of the opposing team. No Recall Knowledge checks are needed to know the
abilities and spells used by creatures of the opposing team.
