  ## Limitations
  ### Hit Points
  After the 5th level, characters no longer gain hit points associated with level advancement. Boosts from feats like Toughness still accumulate to the characters' hit points total. In addition to feats, abilities can grant temporary or permanent hit points.
 </column>
</columns>

> **Why the Change?** Limiting hit points is essential for maintaining the sense of a grim and perilous world, and vulnerability in combat is a massive part of that equation. Capping hit points for PCs and NPCs reduces the duration of combat and keeps the stakes high for everyone. As skills advance and abilities increase,, measuring one's opponents and the situation preceding combat becomes more critical. Abilities and items above level 10 are extremely rare and outside the bounds of acceptable threats, best dealt with on a tactical and military level rather than direct encounters.

## Conditions

### Wounded

You have been seriously injured during a fight. Anytime you lose the dying
condition, you become wounded 1 if you were not already wounded. If you are
already wounded, increase your wounded value by 1 instead. If you gain the
dying condition while you are wounded, increase your dying value by your
wounded value. When someone Treats Wounds on you, they take a status penalty to
the Medicine check equal to your wounded value. You also gain the limping
condition.

To reduce your wounded value by 1, you must spend a full night's rest in safe
and comfortable conditions under the care of an ally trained in Medicine or
Survival using healer's tools, and succeed at a flat check with a DC equal to
10 + your wounded value. On a failure, your wounded condition remains
unchanged, but you gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the following night's check.

In some high-magic campaigns, the GM may allow *restoration* or a similar spell
with the healing trait of 2nd rank or higher to reduce the wounded condition by
1 without requiring a full night's rest and medical assistance.

### Limping

You are limping, and your Speed takes a -10-foot status penalty (minimum 5
feet). Limping requires a full night's rest and successful Treat Wounds to
remove the condition. If an ally successfully Aids a limping character's
movement for a round, that character ignores this penalty until the start of
its next turn.
