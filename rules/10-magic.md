# Magic

## Auras, Emanations and Line of Effect

Auras, and other emanation effects, are an exception to the normal Line of
Effect rules. This means that when creating effects like auras and spells like
*bless* or *detect magic*, their effect can pass through walls, doors, and
other objects that completely block visibility. However, if part of the effect
or an additional effect needs to target creatures, objects, or effects within
the aura or emanation, that effect requires Line of Effect *(Player Core,
pg. 426).*

Some auras and emanations have auditory (*Player Core, pg. 452*) and visual
(*Player Core, pg. 454*) traits. These traits add additional requirements that
the target can hear or see the effect, and require some form of Line of Effect.
The GM may require a Line of Effect based on the intent of the spell.

> This is a departure from core rules to make magic more flexible and
> interesting, and give magic theory a logical depth, less restrained by
> circumstance. In my games, Auras are like a radiation of magical or divine
> energy rather than bound to the point of origin’s perception. For example, a
> Bless Spell will continue to bless allies even if they are in another room
> behind closed doors, and a Detect Magic will locate source of magic through
> thick walls, revealing potential hidden room. Magic fields are also not
> horizontal circles but globes with verticality, so floors above and below
> will also be included in the emanation.
>
> The second part of the rule change guarantees that some effects, such as
> Champion Aura reactions targeting allies or hostile creatures, cannot happen
> without Line of Effect. A Champion cannot prevent damage taken by an ally
> who is in another room, through a closed door, even if the Aura’s magic field
> extends to them.

## Spells with the Concentrate Trait

Every spell with the concentrate trait requires vocal incantations with a
raised voice (65-75 dB), identified as spellcasting. If the spell requires one
action to Cast a Spell all it takes is a command word or two; two action
spells require a short sentence; and at three actions it is two or three lines
of verse (similar to a haiku). If a spell also has the subtle trait, the
required vocal chants can be made as a barely registered whisper (25-30 dB)
and can be hidden in regular speech.

A raised voice used to Cast a Spell can be easily recognized at 240 feet when
directed at its targets, or 120 feet in an enclosed environment that muffles
sound such as twisting corridors, or up to 15 feet if it passes through thin
construction or closed doors. Note that the sound still carries at far
distances but may require a Perception or Recall Knowledge check to recognize
as spellcasting. A whisper used to Cast a Spell with a subtle trait and not
part of an ongoing conversation can be recognized at a 5-feet distance.

The verbal words of power are universally recognized via Identify Spell and do
not belong to a specific language. You cannot Cast a Spell using words in a
dead language to make it impossible for others to detect or identify the
spell.

## Spells with the Manipulate Trait

Every spell with the manipulate trait requires full motion of the upper body
and the arms and is identifiable as spellcasting unless cleverly hidden in a
performative act. This is why having the Grappled condition imposes a DC 5 flat
check risk of disruption of spellcasting and why the Restrained condition
restricts casting spells with the manipulate trait. Full motion of the upper
body and the arms may take the form of broad swipes or drawing symbols in the
air, direct pointing at the target, or similar acts. Line of Effect must
exist between the end of your arms and the target (so you cannot point at a
target if your arms are held behind a thick wall between you and the target).
The arm’s hand does not need to be free and can be wielding weapons or holding
other objects that do not restrict the full motion of the arm.

If a spell also has the subtle trait, the required full motion of the arm
either becomes a careful positioning of only the palm or fingers or can be
hidden in casual body behavior. Note that even if the spell has the subtle
trait and no longer requires the display of full body motion, this does not
remove the penalties imposed by the Grappled and the Restrained conditions as
such conditions make it very difficult to be in total control of your fine
motions.

## Rituals

Rituals are available to spellcasters using the pre-Remaster books. Although
*summon undead* is now a spell, the *summon undead* rituals remain as a method
of getting permanent undead minions.

All Rituals are now of uncommon rarity or higher.
