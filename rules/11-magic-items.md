# Magic Items

## Identifying Magical Properties

Fundamental runes are widely known to all adventurers, merchants, and craftsmen
as a basic form of permanent magic, and the runes (unless intentionally
hidden) are easy to recognize. Players do not need to identify magic to know
its properties if a runic item has only fundamental runes. They can estimate
the item's base price when they attempt to Assess Value before negotiating a
sale or purchase.

If the item has property runes, special properties, or unique powers activated
with an interaction, command, or envision, it must be adequately identified
before its latent magical powers can be awakened and understood. Until it is
identified, such an item only applies its fundamental and property runes that
the players have encountered before.

> **Why the Change?** The new requirement to "awaken" property runes and
> unique powers is intended to keep players from identifying items by simply
> using them in Foundry VTT. Until an object is successfully identified, it
> will be exchanged with a dummy item with only its materials and fundamental
> runes configured.

## Repairing Overcharged Items

The rules for overcharging magical items such as wands and staves consider
them to be broken like a physical object, such as a shield would crack or bend,
all repaired through Crafting. This approach is simple but sets the wrong
assumptions and lacks flavor. Instead of using Repair activity to repair
overcharged items, use Repair Overcharge.

### Repair Overcharge | TRAINED VARIOUS SKILLS ACTIVITY

[EXPLORATION] [MANIPULATE]
You spend 10 minutes examining the aura of a magic wand or staff broken
through overcharging. You use simple incantations to ease the strained magical
energies so the item can be used again. Attempt a check using a skill
corresponding to one of the item's esoteric traditions (Arcana, Nature,
Occultism, or Religion). The GM sets the DC, but it’s usually about the same
DC to Repair a given item. You can’t Repair a destroyed item.

**Success** The item is no longer broken.

**Failure** The item remains broken.

**Critical Failure** As a failure, and you also accidentally trigger an
overcharge of the item. If this is its second overcharge on the same day, the
item is destroyed.
