# Downtime Activities

## Befriend a Local

[CONCENTRATE] [DOWNTIME] [LINGUISTIC] [SECRET]

You spend approximately 4 to 6 hours in the company of an NPC whose
companionship you value to strengthen your friendship bonds with them. You can
always roll a Diplomacy check to determine your degree of success when you try
to Befriend a Local. Still, you can attempt another skill check of your choice
to engage that NPC in an area of expertise they might be interested in. For
example, if you seek to befriend a local smith, you could instead roll a
Crafting check to make friends. Or if you seek to befriend an herbalist, you
could attempt a Medicine or Nature check. If you use a skill that is a poor
choice for the NPC, the GM may treat the outcome of your check as one degree of
success worse.

Regardless of your skill, the DC is 20, or the Will DC of the creature,
whichever is higher. Once you make the check, the NPC becomes immune to
further attempts to befriend them for one month.

**Critical Success** You befriend the NPC, and that NPC particularly favors
your company. If the NPC is a merchant, their goods and services are discounted
by 10%. If the NPC is not a merchant, their friendship grants you a +1
circumstance bonus to Society and Diplomacy checks or other skill they are at
least trained in if you ask for their aid as part of downtime activities, as
long as they favor you. These benefits are permanent as long as you don’t take
actions that jeopardize this friendship (as determined by the GM). Once you
have permanently gained an NPC’s favor in this way, the GM might decide the NPC
helps you in other ways.

**Success** Per critical success, except the NPC’s favor lasts for several
weeks equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of 1 week).

**Failure** The NPC does not favor you.

**Critical Failure** The NPC is offended or insulted by something you did. You
no longer gain the effects of an ongoing success or critical success with that
NPC, and the DC to use *Befriend a Local* with that NPC in the future
increases by 5.

> **Befriending vs. Impressions** Make and Impression is a faster and easier
> way to improve the target's attitude than Befriend a Local; however, the
> improved impression is not permanent. Impressions usually last only for the
> current social interaction. In comparison, befriending lasts until a
> friendship is tarnished, and if properly maintained over time (by repeating
> at least once a month) it may last for a lifetime.

## Gather Raw Materials

[DOWNTIME] [MANIPULATE]

You work to gather raw materials from the surrounding environment. This works
exactly like Earn Income, except it uses different skills, and you receive raw
materials rather than gold.

The GM determines what tasks can be performed to Gather Raw Materials in the
environment, the tasks' levels, and what kinds of raw materials they will
yield based on the abundance and worth of potential raw materials in the area
and the straightforwardness of gathering them. Usually, the skill is Nature or
Survival outdoors, and Society and Crafting in an urban environment. A specific
Lore may also apply and lower the DC by 2 as usual.

At the GM's discretion, you may need to have specific tools on hand; for
example, cutting down a tree for its wood would typically require an axe. Some
Gather Raw Materials tasks may only be available for a limited time, deplete
after the available raw resources have been gathered, or be subject to other
natural variations.

> **Sample Gather Raw Materials Tasks**
> **Untrained** collect river stones, scavenge animal bones
> **Trained** chop and treat timber, pan for gold
> **Expert** harvest organs, collect delicate herbs
> **Master** collect a phoenix's feathers, draw sap from an arboreal regent
> **Legendary** harvest a sun orchid, congeal energies from a ley line

> **Impromptu Gathering**
>
> Unlike most downtime activities, you can also Gather Raw Materials during a
> day you engage in Exploration. However, doing so is less efficient and runs
> the risk of imperfect preservation or haphazard storage, causing them to go
> to waste. Roll to Gather Raw Materials as part of 1 hour of work. Your work
> yields 10% of the raw materials gained from Gathering Raw Materials for a day
> of downtime. These materials spoil or become unusable if not supplied to
> create an item within 3 days of being gathered.
>
> However, when gathering from a particularly notable source, such as the
> remains of a dragon, the GM may wave this restriction; additionally, such
> sources may be more lucrative, yielding instead 20% or even 50% of raw
> materials that would typically be gathered with a day of downtime in that 1
> hour. This exceptionally valuable gathering only lasts so long - usually
> either until a specific value representing the most high-value raw materials
> has been gathered or until a certain amount of time has passed and what
> remains begins to spoil or lose its purity. After that, if gathering more raw
> materials from the source is still possible, it proceeds at the 10% rate.
>
> After making the check, you can spend additional hours Gathering Raw
> Materials in the same manner as you would otherwise spend additional days.
> Still, you cannot exceed 50% of the raw material typically gathered with a
> day of downtime in that 1 hour.

## Training

[ DOWNTIME] [SECRET]
**Requirement:** You must have at least one week of downtime before the event
you are training for and be aware of it ahead of time.

At the end of your training period, make a skill check against a Simple DC for
your proficiency rank or the DC for the level of the upcoming event whichever
is higher. The GM may adjust the DC based on its particular difficulty. You
may use any appropriate skill, usually athletics for sporting events and
Gather Information or Recall Knowledge for intellectual or social events.
However, the GM can allow any appropriate skill or activity based on the
event. If you spent a month or more in preparation for the event, increase the
bonus granted by 1 on a success or critical success. You may only have the
effects of one bonus or penalty from training at a time, when you roll another
Training check, you use the new result and ignore any previous results.

**Critical Success** You gain a +2 status bonus on skill checks related to
completing the event you trained for during the length of the event. You must
spend at least one hour a day doing light exercise, study, or practice as
appropriate for the event or lose this bonus. Once the event is over, you lose
this bonus.

**Success** As critical success but the status bonus is +1.

**Failure** You gain no benefit, if you still have at least a week until the
event, you may try again.

**Critical Failure** You suffer an injury, fall a rabbit hole of false
information, or otherwise are hindered in your training. You gain a -1 Status
penalty on all skill checks related to completing your event. If you have at
least 2 weeks of downtime left before your event, you may try again after one
week has passed. Treating that as the time at which you began training.
Otherwise, this penalty lasts until the event ends or a week passes, whichever
comes first.

## Research

[CONCENTRATE] [DOWNTIME] [LINGUISTIC]

You spend time researching a creature, object, place, or event. To research a
topic, you must have access to a suitable repository of knowledge, such as a
library. More obscure topics may need a more expansive place to research, and
some topics may not be able to be researched at all, at the GM’s discretion.

Suppose you spend 1 day researching the topic. In that case, you benefit from a
+1 circumstance bonus on your next Recall Knowledge check related to the topic
within the next week, as long as the check does not require expert or better
proficiency. If you spend 1 week studying the topic, the benefit applies to
all Recall Knowledge checks related to the topic within the next week, as long
as the check does not require expert or better proficiency. If you spend 1
month studying the topic, you permanently gain the Additional Lore skill feat.
Still, the additional lore cannot increase above Trained unless you spend an
additional 2 months studying for each proficiency level above Trained, up to
the maximum your level allows as listed in the feat.
