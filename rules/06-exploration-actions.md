# Exploration Actions

### Assess Value or Negotiate

[CONCENTRATE] [EXPLORATION] **[SECRET]**
You examine an item for 1 minute, usually when negotiating with a trader to
sell or purchase it, and attempt to find its monetary value. If the item has
properties not yet identified, such as alchemical, magical, or occult effects,
you take a -4 circumstance penalty on the check. The GM determines which
skills apply. The DC depends on the interaction with the prospective buyer or
seller:

- If the buyer or seller can easily afford to pay for the item and agrees to
  the valuation of the item, the DC is based on the level of the item and its
  rarity; increase the DC by 2 for Uncommon items, 5 for Rare items, and 10 for
  Unique items. If the person buying or selling the item is friendly or
  helpful towards you, they might provide you with an accurate price outright,
  give you a 10% discount or overpay, or you get a +2 status bonus to the
  check.
- If the buyer or seller is unable to pay the full price, runs a business based
  on profit from buying low and selling high, or considers the price unfair,
  they may decide to haggle. If they do so, the DC is based on Will DC +2 for
  individual merchants and small shops, or Mercantile Lore DC (10 + Trade
  Bonus) for caravans, guilds, and organized operations. Haggling is a general
  shift of the tone that, if taken to extremes, can sour friendships or have
  you thrown out of a shop, and the time of the negotiations changes to 10
  minutes. Increase the difficulty modifier to +4 if the negotiation is a
  particularly difficult one, or the buyer or seller is unfriendly or hostile.

**Critical Success** As success and your excellent assessment allow you to
negotiate the price in your favor by 5 percent plus 2 percent for each
proficiency level of the used skill (7 percent if Trained, 9 percent if
Expert, 11 percent if Master, 13 percent if Legendary). You cannot decrease
the value below 10%. You cannot sell above 90% unless the item is considered
Rare or Unique or has collector value, and the prospective buyer believes they
can resell it at a higher value.

**Success** You find the item's base price if you didn’t know it. You can also
find the exact selling value of the item. Consult the table below to find the
appropriate percentage and applicable modifiers based on the nature of the
item or the market where it is sold.

**Failure:** You devise a vague approximation of the item's selling price that
is lower than it actually is. **The other party who sells to you or purchases
the item from you can change the price by 5 percent in their favor.

**Critical Failure** You make an utterly false estimation of the item’s value
way below its actual value. The other party who sells to you or purchases the
item from you can change the price in their favor by 10 percent plus 2 percent
for each proficiency level of their applicable skill (usually Arcana, Crafting,
Diplomacy, Society or Thievery). The negotiated price of the item cannot be
lower than 10 percent of the base price. This is the seller or buyer’s final
offer.

**Special** The following skills can be used to Assess Value, depending on the
nature of the examined item. If you are trained in a Lore skill directly
related to the reviewed item, you have a +2 circumstance bonus to the check.
Sometimes, you can get the GM’s permission to use a different but related
skill, usually against a higher DC than average.

- **Arcana, Nature, Occultism, or Religion:** magical items of the skill's
  associated magic tradition.
- **Crafting:** mundane, alchemical, and mechanical items.
- **Nature:** precious materials, rare minerals, crystals, and gemstones used
  to store value or used as magic cost or crafting components
- **Occultism:** items of unknown or supernatural origin that may not originate
  from this world and may have a greater purpose
- **Religion:** sacred items of divine worship or anathema of great importance
  to followers of that faith or collectors.
- **Thievery:** contraband and black market goods, stolen goods, and fakes.

> **Retries**: When making an Assess Price or a Negotiate check, you can use
> Hero Points as usual for exploration activities. Once that option is
> exhausted or not used, you may retry an Assess Price by paying an Expert
> (with the required skill at least at expert proficiency) to make an
> independent assessment, or again once you level up. You may retry a Negotiate
> with another buyer or seller, or again after one week of market price
> fluctuations have passed.

### Hide Item

[EXPLORATION] [MANIPULATE] **[SECRET]**
**Requirements** trained in Stealth or Survival

You spent 10 minutes camouflaging an item of up to 1 Bulk so it blends with
the environment and becomes hidden rather than observed. The GM decides
whether there is an appropriate environment or sufficient materials for the
camouflage and whether it provides cover or greater cover to the item.

The GM then rolls in secret a Stealth check if the item is hidden indoors or
Survival if the item is hidden outdoors and compares the result to the
Perception DC of each creature that Seeks hidden items or snares, applying +2
to the roll for cover or +4 for greater cover.
