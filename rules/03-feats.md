# Barbarian

### Wounded Rage FEAT 4

[BARBARIAN]

**Trigger** You take damage and are capable of entering a rage.

---
You roar in pain, awakening the rage within you. You Rage. **Instead of
gaining temporary hit points as you Rage, you reduce the damage taken with the
same amount.**

> If the damage takes you to 0 hit points, you cannot Rage in response. The
> main reason feat was considered less than optimal since Legacy. This change
> makes the feat more useful by preventing you from dropping to 0 hit points
> in fringe situations.

### Barreling Charge ◆◆ FEAT 4

[BARBARIAN] [FLOURISH]

**Prerequisites** trained in Athletics

---
You rush forward, **charge through solid obstacles without hesitation and**
moving enemies aside to reach your foe. You Stride **once or twice.**

**If you stride twice, once during your movement, if your movement passes
through or ends adjacent to a door, window, fence, wall, or similar obstacle,
you can attempt an Athletics check to Force Open the obstacle with a +1
circumstance bonus to the roll; on a failure, your movement ends at that
obstacle.**

**If you Stride only once, you can attempt to** move through your enemies'
spaces, and make a melee Strike. Roll an Athletics check and compare the
result to the Fortitude DC of each creature whose space you attempt to move
through during your Stride, moving through its space on a success but ending
your movement before entering its space on a failure. **You can then Strike,
regardless of how your Stride ended.**You can use Barreling Charge to Burrow,
Climb, Fly, or Swim instead of Stride, as long as you have the corresponding
movement type.

> Both Bashing Charge and Barreling Charge are feats that see no table use due
> to their limited usability, and cannot compete with the universal appeal of
> Sudden Charge. Bashing Charge is almost always a waste. Barreling charge has
> appeal because it is a way for the Barbarian to Tumble Through multiple
> opponents, but still this is a limited appeal. This unification as a single
> feat is an attempt to create a “utility toolbox” charge option that may still
> be weaker than Sudden Charge appeal, while not changing the balance of the
> class.

### Cleave ↺ FEAT 4

[BARBARIAN] [RAGE]

Trigger **Your melee Strike kills a creature or knocks it unconscious, and
another foe is within reach of a melee Strike.**

---
You swing clear through one foe and into another. Make a melee Strike against
the second foe.

> Adjacency-limited effects like Cleave or Axe critical specialization effect
> suffer from limited usability due to how combat in Pathfinder 2e works
> (fighting multiple weak enemies who swarm together is less frequent than a
> boss with a minion or two, and they rarely bundle together instead of
> flanking you). While the Axe critical specialization effect is homebrewed
> with a fallback damage effect and is free with class progression, Cleave is a
> feat pick and should not suffer because the game doesn’t encourage adjacency,
> in order to make it appealing to players. Also, trigger is reworded to avoid
> minor exploit using enemies with Regeneration that constantly recover from 0
> hit points.

# Bard

### Melodious Spell ◆ FEAT 4

[BARD]

---
You subtly weave your spellcasting into a performance **that draws attention to
you but doesn’t look like spellcasting.** **Make a Performance or Stealth
(whichever is higher) against the target’s Perception DC, or two checks if the
spell has the manipulate trait and the target is observing you. If you succeed
in the check (or both checks) and** **the following action you use is to Cast a
Spell, the spell gains the subtle trait, hiding the words of power,** sparks of
magic, and other manifestations that would usually give away your spellcasting.
The trait hides only the spell’s spellcasting actions and manifestations, not
its effects, so an observer might still see a ray streak out from you or see
you vanish into thin air.

As part of using Melodious Spell, you can choose to Create a Diversion,
Demoralize, Perform, or Request. If you do so, the spell you cast must have the
auditory, emotion, sonic, or visual trait to gain the subtle trait.

> This is a needed nerf to bring the feat back to Legacy rules. Same reasoning
> as Wizard’s Conceal Spell.

# Wizard

### Conceal Spell ◆ FEAT 2

[CONCENTRATE] [SPELLSHAPE] [WIZARD] [WITCH]

---
By shaping the magical energies and parameters of your spells all in your head
through sheer concentration, you can simplify the incantations and gestures
needed to cast the spell, leaving them barely noticeable. **Make a Stealth
check or spell attack roll (using whichever modifier is higher) against the
target’s Perception DC,
or two checks if the spell has the manipulate trait and the target is
observing you. If you succeed in the check (or both checks) and** **the
following action you use is to Cast a Spell, the spell gains the subtle
trait, hushing the words of power,** hiding the shining runes, sparks of magic,
and other manifestations that would usually give away your spell casting. The
trait hides only the spell’s spellcasting actions and manifestations, not its
effects, so an observer might still see a ray streak out from you or see you
vanish into thin air.

> This is a needed nerf to bring the feat back to Legacy rules. Allowing any
> spells to become Subtle has unrestrained unforeseen consequences of
> campaign-breaking proportions when combined with intelligent use of the
> Stealth and Ambush rules, such as every spellcaster of the party casting a
> full spell at enemies before initiative is started because casting does not
> trigger a start of encounter since their casting and manifestation cannot be
> observed. However, I recognize that the old design required Wizards to be
> good at Deception, which was a harsh spread of their few attribute
> advancements, so the rules have been changed to rely on Stealth (Dexterity
> being generally secondary preferred attribute) or Spell Attack which the
> Wizard excels at.

> Conceal Spell is intentionally designed to be hard to pull off. Players
> should use Create a Diversion to get temporary -4 circumstance penalty to
> Perception DC of all fooled by the diversion; or seek and use Greater Cover
> to get +4 circumstance bonus to Stealth checks, or Total Cover to avoid
> making a secondary check for manipulate trait spells.

### Silent Spell FEAT 4

[CONCENTRATE] [SPELLSHAPE] [WIZARD]
**Prerequisites** Conceal Spell

---
You’ve learned how to cast many of your spells without speaking the words of
power you would normally need to provide. **When you use an action to Conceal
Spell, you automatically get a success on the first Stealth check or spell
attack roll.**This makes the spell quieter and allows you to cast it in areas where
sound can’t carry. However, the spell still has visual manifestations, so this
doesn’t make it any less obvious to someone who sees you casting it.

> The feat Silent Spell was reintroduced from Legacy to circumvent the need
> for a check if only one check is needed, or reduce from two checks to one
> check. The cost of this is again two-feat tax, considering the implications
> of silent casting outside of encounter mode.

# General

### Diehard FEAT 1

[GENERAL]

---
It takes more to kill you than most. You die from the dying condition at dying
5, rather than dying 4. **Massive Damage kills you outright only when it equals
or exceeds three times your maximum hit points.**

> Many GMs ignore the Massive Damage rules, but for those who don’t, Massive
> Damage can happen during levels 1-2 and anytime due to critical falling
> distance. Diehard has been improved, and the Massive Damage threshold now
> allows some extreme falling distance to result in dying instead of instant
> death cases. This is useful in certain campaigns.

# General Skill

### Dubious Knowledge FEAT 1

[GENERAL] [SKILL]
**Prerequisites** trained in a skill with the Recall Knowledge action

---
You’re a treasure trove of information, but not all of it comes from reputable
sources. When you fail (but don’t critically fail) a Recall Knowledge check
using any skill, **you learn a small fact about the subject unrelated to your
question (such as a creature’s hunting behavior rather than its name or
weakest saving throw). The first time you fail (but don’t critically fail) a
Recall Knowledge check, you can make one additional attempt at a one-step
higher difficulty adjustment.**

> Dubious Knowledge can be annoying when it forces GMs to give improv false
> information that will immediately be recognized as such. Instead, it is
> clarified as unrelated information that the GM can make useful or not but
> does not answer the question asked, and there’s a solid benefit of retrying
> once a failed Recall Knowledge which makes this a desired feat for any Recall
> Knowledge build.

### Hobnobber FEAT 1

[GENERAL] [SKILL]
**Prerequisites** trained in Diplomacy

---
You are skilled at learning information through conversation. **You have a +1
circumstance bonus to the Gather Information exploration activity**, and it
takes you half as long as normal (typically reducing the time to 1 hour). If
you’re a master in Diplomacy and roll a critical failure to Gather Information,
you get a failure instead. A rumor you learn with Gather Information is not
guaranteed to be accurate.

> Hobnobber is almost useless for campaigns where time is not on the clock for
> collecting information. There are no feats that give a circumstance bonus to
> Gather Information, so Hobnobber is being upgraded from a useless to a useful
> feat for social campaigns.

### Read Lips [1]

[GENERAL] [SKILL]
**Prerequisites** trained in Society

---
You can read the lips of others nearby who you can see. The language read must
be one that you know. When you’re at your leisure, you can do this
automatically. In encounter mode or when attempting a more difficult feat of
lipreading, you’re fascinated and off-guard during each round in which you
focus on lip movements, and you must succeed at a Society check (DC determined
by the GM) to successfully read someone’s lips. **If you also have the Sign
Language feat, you can register the casting of spells with the subtle trait.
When you register the casting of a spell with a subtle trait, you can
immediately call for initiative and use a reaction to Recognize Spell or
Counterspell as if the spell only has no subtle trait for you.**

> While Read Lips is useful, flavorful counters to Subtle spells must be
> integrated by spreading them among this and Sign Language feats. Note that
> Sign Language is only half of the counter of Subtle spells; you also need
> Sign Language to gain a full counter. Having both this and Sign Language is
> required to close a loophole where two characters each have each feat and
> achieve the same result. This is also necessary because Sign Language can
> only catch a small subset of subtle spells. Usually, those made subtle with
> Conceal Spell feat.

### Sign Language FEAT 1

[GENERAL] [SKILL]

**Prerequisites** trained in Society

---
You learn the sign languages associated with the languages you know, allowing
you to sign and understand signs. Sign languages typically require both hands
to convey more complex concepts, which are visual rather than auditory.
**Sign Language is a secret language that only other creatures with Sign
Language can understand perfectly.** **You can also register the casting of
spells with both manipulate and subtle traits. When you register the casting
of a spell with a subtle trait, you can immediately call for initiative and
use a reaction to Recognize Spell or Counterspell as if the spell only has no
subtle trait for you.**

**Standard** sign language is difficult to understand during combat due to the
level of attention needed. **However, you** **also learn battle sign
language. You can use a manipulate action to convey complex tactical commands
and information during combat with one free hand to allies who can see you and
have Sign Language.** Sign language is hard to
use in areas of low visibility, just like speech is difficult in a noisy
environment.

> It’s a known issue that Sign Language is extremely low usability because
> Message is widely available, so to make it useful again, two major design
> directions were explored. This change interacts with the spell concealment
> capabilities of the Subtle trait by adding a useful way for paranoid
> characters to recognize Subtle casting, which otherwise has no counter in the
> game. Note that Sign Language is only half of the counter of Subtle spells;
> you also need to Read Lips to gain a full counter. Sign Language only
> detects a small subset of spells that have the Subtle spell, and those made
> subtle with the Conceal Spell feat. The other direction is to combine Sign
> Language with Battle Sign Language for the few useful cases where non-verbal
> communication between non-spellcasters is needed, directly benefiting
> squad-based roleplay among martial characters.
