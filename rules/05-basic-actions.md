# Basic Actions

## Core Rulebook Actions

### Aid (pg. 463)

The default DC for an Aid check is DC 15 if the situation happens during
Downtime or Narrative Exploration mode, or DC 20 if performed in Exploration
mode under danger, or Encounter mode (such as Combat or during a Sneak). The
GM has the final say if the action you are Aiding will use DC 15 or DC 20, and
you can know this before you attempt to Aid.

When you Aid an ally attempting a check with a minimum proficiency requirement
of Master or Legendary, the DC to Aid changes to 25 if Master is required, or
30 if Legendary is needed. This is typically only important for Aiding a
Disable a Device against high-level Hazards, but also applies to any check
where the GM mentions that the aided creature must have a minimum proficiency
tier.

When Aiding an ally against intelligent, hostile creatures capable of
detecting the intent of your Aid action, after you attempt to Aid using a
particular check or creative approach, if you make a similar attempt with the
same type of skill or attack check and with the same approach until the end of
the encounter, the DC becomes Very Hard (DC +5, typically 25). To avoid the
increase in DC, describe a different approach, or change the skill used or the
weapon used for the attack roll.

> **What is Changed?** Remaster rules made Aid extremely easy to pull off
> changing its base DC from Expert to Trained. While this is helpful for new
> players with low-tier characters to feel Aid is useful and remember to use
> it, it becomes way too easy to get critical success on Aid way too early,
> which used to be benefit for the mid to late tiers, now available to low to
> mid tiers and becomes free bonus at high tiers. It also simplified Aid so
> much it no longer needs support feats or other specialization as before for
> guaranteed benefits on earlier levels. This rule carefully integrates both
> the old DC and the new DC by giving the GM control, and making the DC to Aid
> during combat to the Legacy one and ensures that easy critical results do not
> disbalance the game in low to lower-mid tiers. Treat the Remaster DC for Aid
> as a Very Easy Aid (since it is done with no pressure or performance
> requirements, and the target is likely willing).

### Climb (pg. 234)

The Climb action adds a special ruling

**Special** While the Climb action requires both hands free to move up, down,
or across an incline, you can use up to one hand to make Interact or Attack
actions as long as you used at least one Climb action on the same turn to
sustain your grip on the incline, and you may choose not to move when using
that Climb action. You can use both hands only if you use a climbing tool that
is strapped to you and prevents you from falling; if you do so, the Climb
action to sustain your grip is always a failure.

> **What is Changed?** Climb is expanded with an additional option to
> interact, as long as the climbing process is sustained like Fly and Swim
> requires one action to sustain.

### Coerce (pg. 239)

When you attempt to Coerce a creature, if it has one or more allies present
and aware of that attempt that are not targets of the same attempt (such as
with the Group Coercion feat), the allies may speak up to oppose the attempt as
a reaction. This imposes a -2 circumstance penalty to the Coerce check,
potentially causing it to fail. The penalty increases to -4 if both the target
and the ally opposing the attempt are indifferent, unfriendly, or hostile. This
opposition counts as a hostile action.

> The following ruling is made to fix the irrelevance of Group Coercion feat.
> In Remaster, Make an Impression includes a penalty clause when used to target
> groups, and Group Impression removes the penalty and increases the group
> size. Coercion is a potentially hostile social action lacking such a clause,
> and it is unclear what prevents its use against multiple targets or just the
> “leader” among the targets, making Group Coercion useless. This ruling gives
> the target's allies an option to oppose attempts by Coerce, which would be
> denied to them if Group Coerce included them as targets. This makes the feat
> very good for coercing a group of hostile creatures to avoid needless combat,
> give up their positions, or do something against their collective will.

> Although the ruling is intended for Coerce and Group Coerce feats, the
> concept of vocal opposition can be applied to Make an Impression or Request
> checks. Since there is no Group Request feat, in the case of Request each
> target creature must be Requested individually, covering for all their added
> provisions or alterations to the request, if any. Once they agree to the
> Request, they lose their ability to oppose the same Request targeting their
> allies. Players benefit if fewer people consult the target of their Request.

### Interact  (pg. 268, 416)

You can not swap items (stow an item from your hand and draw another) as a
single Interact action. The rules on pages 268 and 416 that list swapping
items do not apply.

> The Remaster Project completely broke interaction integrity in the name of
> modern player comfort by combining stowing and drawing items (known as
> swapping) as a single Interact action. Meanwhile, drawing and stowing items
> still cost an Interact action, but for video-game quality of life, when done
> in order, the two actions cost one action instead of two for no reason but
> player comfort. This simplified a lot of in-combat decisions of dropping items
> to a videogame logic where items “stick’ magnetically on your body instead of
> taking time to stow correctly. This is unacceptable for my games; legacy
> rules are used for realism and system integrity.

## New Actions

### Choke a Creature ◆

[ATTACK] [NONLETHAL] [INCAPACITATION]
**Requirements** You have at least one hand free or a weapon with a choke
trait (such as garotte). The target is your size or smaller and has an exposed
throat that can be choked.

You apply firm pressure on an opponent's throat and suffocate them. Attempt a
Grapple check with a -2 circumstance penalty. You don't take this penalty if
you are already grabbing your target or using a weapon with a choke trait
(such as Garotte).

**Critical Success** As a success, the opponent is also restrained, loses 2
rounds of air, and automatically takes damage from one unarmed Strike inherent
to hands used to choke or a weapon with the choke trait (such as garotte).
Until the start of your next turn, your opponent is gagged and loses 1
additional round of air if they take damage or fail a saving throw.

**Success** The opponent is grabbed and suffocating until the end of your next
turn unless you move or your opponent Escapes. Reduce the remaining air by 1
round (A creature can sustain up to 5 + its Constitution modifier rounds of
air before dropping unconscious and starting suffocating; see Player Core 1,
pg. 437). Whenever your opponent attempts to speak (including casting spells
with verbal components or activating items with command components) while
choked, they must succeed at a DC 11 flat check or lose 1 additional round of
air.

**Failure** You fail to maintain a good grip on your opponent's throat. If
your target is already choked, they managed to breathe, restoring their air
supply by 2 rounds, but cannot exceed their maximum (5 + its Constitution
modifier). If you grab or restrain your opponent, they may immediately attempt
to Escape as a reaction.

**Critical Failure** As a failure, and if you grabbed or restrained the
opponent, they automatically Escaped. You are off-guard toward the target
until the start of your next turn.

**Special** If you are a master in Athletics, you can choke a target one size
larger than you using two hands or targets of your size or smaller with one
hand. If you are legendary, you only need one hand for targets one size larger
than you.

> Garotte mentioned in this action does not exist in Legacy or Remaster books,
> but can be introduced on demand. For it to work with Choke and suffocation, a
> new weapon trait, choke, is introduced.

### Descending Jump ◆◆

![]()
Leap 5 feet horizontally to a lower elevation, then attempt an Athletics check
to absorb the force of your landing. This DC is 10 plus the feet of distance
between your starting and ending elevation. The DC might be increased or
decreased due to the situation, as determined by the GM.

**Critical Success** Treat your fall as 10 feet shorter, and you can Step as
part of absorbing the force of your landing.
**Success** As critical success, but only treat your fall as 5 feet shorter.
**Failure** You Leap normally but you fall prone.
**Critical Failure** You Leap normally and land badly. As failure, but treat
your fall as 10 feet longer.
**Special** Any bonus that applies to both High Jump and Long Jump applies to
Descending Jump.

### Drag ◆

[ATTACK] [MANIPULATE]
**Requirements** You have a creature grabbed or restrained, and the target
can’t be more than one size larger than you.

You attempt to pull a creature you have a hold on. Attempt an Athletics check
to Grapple the target with the following effects instead of the usual effects.
While using the Drag action, movement or your target’s movement does not end
the grabbed condition. You are off-guard while dragging a creature.

If the creature is unconscious, the action loses the Attack trait, and you get
critical success automatically. If you and an unconscious creature share the
same space, you grab that creature as part of the Drag action, and the
requirement to have it grabbed or restrained does not apply.

In an appropriate environment, you can Climb, Crawl, Fly, or Swim instead of
Stride by choosing to Drag as a 2-action activity; at this GM's discretion,
this may require an Athletics check, with the customary +4 circumstance bonus
when Climbing or Swimming if you have an appropriate Speed. Some very difficult
terrain or uneven terrain may not allow to drag a creature through it, check
Lift basic action for such cases.

**Critical Success** As a success, twice. If you are legendary in Athletics,
you can perform it thrice.

**Success** Stride 5ft, and move the target 5 feet in the same direction, or
you and the target switch positions. The target remains grabbed or restrained
by you until the start of your next turn.

**Critical Failure** You lose your balance, fall, and land prone. The target is
no longer grappled or restrained by you.

**Special** Effects that alter the size requirement of Grapple and Reposition,
such as Titan Wrestler, also apply to Drag. If you critically fail the
Athletics check using a weapon with the Grapple trait, the Grapple trait allows
you to avoid falling prone by dropping the weapon.

> Drag has been updated to incorporate the old custom Reposition rules for
> unconscious bodies since PF2 Remaster now includes its own Reposition action.

### Gag a Creature ◆

[ATTACK]
**Requirement** You have at least one hand free. The target is your size or
smaller and has an easily accessible mouth.

You reach out around your target's head and cover its mouth before it can
speak. Attempt an Athletics check against the opponent's Reflex DC. You are
off-guard while gagging the creature.

**Critical Success** As a success, but the opponent is also *stunned* 1.

**Success** The opponent gains the gagged condition until the end of your next
turn unless you move or your opponent Escapes the gag. Unless the target is
also grabbed, it has a +2 circumstance bonus to its attempts to Escape your
gagging.

**Critical Failure** If you already had the opponent gagged, they
automatically Escape your gagging. If the opponent has a Bite Strike, it can
Strike against you as a reaction.

**Special** If you are a master in Athletics, you can gag a target one size
larger than you using two hands or targets of your size or smaller with one
hand. If you are legendary, you only need one hand for targets one size larger
than you.

> **New Condition: Gagged**
>
> Your mouth is covered by another creature, giving you the off-guard
> condition. You cannot form clear speech, cast spells with the concentrate
> trait even if they have the subtle trait, or activate items with commands.
> You can make vague muffled sounds only heard within 15 feet, or you can
> spend an action and succeed at a DC 18 flat check to make up to two words
> heard within 30 feet, activate an item with ◆ command, or Cast a Spell with
> up to ◆ action. You can remove the Gagged condition with a successful Escape.
> If you are gagged and restrained at the same time, you must Escape the
> restrained state before you can Escape being gagged.
>
> If you have a Bite Strike, you benefit from a +4 circumstance bonus to
> attack against the creature that is gagging you.

### Kick Object ◆ OR ◆◆

[ATTACK] [MANIPULATE]
**Requirements** You share space with an unattended item.

You kick an item of up to 2 Bulk so it moves in the direction of your choice.
You can make a soft or a hard Kick. If the item is of Negligible bulk, it is
moved 15 feet if kicked softly or 30 feet if kicked hard. If the item is of
Light bulk, it is moved 10 feet if kicked softly or 20 feet if kicked hard. If
the item is of 1 or 2 Bulk, it is moved 5 feet if kicked softly or 10 feet if
kicked hard.

If the item is located in a space shared by a hostile creature, or you and a
hostile creature share the same space, Kick Object costs ◆◆ and you must
Tumble Through its space. If your Tumble Through is successful, you may Kick
an Object during your movement into or out of the space occupied by both the
item and the hostile creature. You can only Kick softly while you Tumble
Through.

The GM can modify these distances, as some items may be easier or harder to
kick due to their size, shape, the material they are made of, or the friction
of the surface if any. For some items, a hard kick should move it at a distance
multiplied by your Strength modifier. For example, a leather ball during a game
of Ruk, a sports game popular in Katapesh, would move 30 feet if kicked softly
and 50 feet multiplied by your Strength modifier if kicked hard. The GM may ask
for a skill check (usually Athletics, Acrobatics, or Sports Lore) if the kicked
item has to land within a goal post guarded by a sentry player.

### Lift ◆◆

[MANIPULATE]
**Requirements** Both of your hands are free

You attempt to lift an object or creature that would cause you to exceed your
maximum Bulk, whether by shrugging a portcullis onto your shoulders or just
lifting a heavy rock. Even if you could normally lift it without a check,
lifting the object puts you over your maximum bulk and causes movement to be
difficult to nearly impossible.

Make an Athletics check against a DC. The DM may adjust the difficulty
depending on things like the conditions of the lift, typically with the Easy or
Hard DC adjustments. Objects without means to be lifted such as handles to
grab, or with irregular shape and center of weight are always at least Hard to
lift. If something is rooted into the ground, such as the foundation of a
building or the roots of a profoundly ancient tree, might use the Very Hard or
even Incredibly Hard DC adjustments.

Lifting an unwilling creature that isn't somehow rendered helpless is nearly
impossible. A DM may allow you to attempt to do it under certain circumstances,
but its DC should be one step harder under such circumstances or use the
aforementioned difficulty adjustments if the DC is already Legendary.

**Critical Success** You lift the object or creature over your head, your
shoulder, or under your arm, whatever is preferred (or possible). You take a
15 foot penalty to your speed, rather than the typical penalty from being
encumbered, to a minimum of 5 feet. You can maintain your grip on the object or
creature for up to a minute, at which point you must use this action and roll
again at the start of your turn at one step of difficulty higher, or drop said
object or creature into the nearest unoccupied space large enough for it to be
set down. If there is no such space, you are pushed away as you drop it into
the nearest unoccupied space until the is room for the object or creature to
be set down.

**Success** As critical success, but the penalty to your speed is 25 feet to a
minimum of 0 feet, and you must take this action and roll again at the start of
your next turn or drop the object or creature.

**Failure** You do not lift the object or creature.

**Critical Failure** As a failure, but you overextend yourself and lose your
balance. You fall prone.

**Sample DCs**

**Trained (DC 15)** Something the same size and weight as you. A heavy large
rock. A medium willing or unconscious creature.

**Expert (DC 20)** Something one size larger than you. A large-sized boulder.
A large tree trunk. A large willing or unconscious creature.

**Master (DC 30)** Something two sizes larger than you. An unconscious or
willing huge creature. A small car.

**Legendary (DC 40)** Something three sizes larger than you. An uprooted house.
A gargantuan willing or unconscious creature.

### Pass To Ally ◆

[MANIPULATE]
**Requirements** You hold an item in your hand, and an ally is within reach

In the heat of combat, you prepare to pass an item to an ally. The item must
be in your hands and not worn on you. Until the start of your next turn, any
ally of your choice can use its reaction or action on its turn to take the
item from your hand. At the moment of exchange, either you or the ally must
have the other one in their hand's reach. Taking the item has the Manipulate
trait. The ally may need to spend an Interact action on its turn to wear or
stow the item before it can free its hand (up to GM's discretion).

You can only Pass To Ally one item at a time unless the GM rules otherwise due
to the item's size, weight, and ease of handling. When passing ammunition, you
usually pass a single container rather than a specific unit of ammunition. If
specific units need to be directly passed, a handful typically holds 4 units of
tiny items.

You can also throw an item to an ally outside of each other's reach who must
spend a reaction to catch the item. Both you and an ally make an Athletics or
Acrobatics check (each player can choose a different skill). The DC of the
check is 15 plus 5 for every 10 feet of distance between you and that ally.
The GM can modify the DC due to concealment or other circumstances. You and the
ally must succeed on the check to catch the item. If you succeed, but the ally
doesn't or cannot use its reaction, the item falls on their feet, and they can
use an action on their turn to take it from the ground. If you fail, the item
is thrown as far as the check result allows.

### Steady ◆

[MOVE]
**Requirements** You are standing on an uneven terrain.

You spend an action to steady your footing on uneven, treacherous ground
momentarily. This allows you to treat failures (but not critical failures) on
Reflex saves to avoid falling as successes until the space you occupy changes
or until the start of your next turn.

### Strap an Item ◆◆◆

[MANIPULATE]
**Requirements** You have at least one hand free. The target is willing,
paralyzed, or unconscious. The target can’t be more than one size larger than
you.

Using a free hand, you or an ally attach an item to one of your hands (or
other appendages that can hold items) so the item will not be dropped
unattended should you fall unconscious. The item cannot have the thrown trait
and must have the means to be strapped. Most Shields come with such strap
attachments, but other items may require custom wear. While an item is
strapped to your hand, it is considered worn, and the hand is no longer free
unless the item allows it (such as Buckler being attached to your forearm so
the hand remains free) or ◆◆ interact action is used to unstrap it with a
free hand, or an ally’s assistance if you have no free hands. A strapped item
cannot be dropped as a free action.
